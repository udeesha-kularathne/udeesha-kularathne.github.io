Sentiment & Crisis Intelligence System

A hybrid NLP and analytics system that transforms large-scale textual reviews and crisis indicators into structured district-level sentiment and risk scores, designed to integrate directly with geospatial digital twins and forecasting engines.

Project Overview

This project serves as the intelligence layer for a larger tourism digital twin ecosystem. Its primary objective is to convert unstructured textual signals (such as reviews and public sentiment) and structured crisis indicators into consistent, district-level scores that can be consumed by downstream analytics and forecasting systems.

Instead of relying on a single NLP model, the system uses a hybrid ensemble architecture built on transformer-based embeddings combined with multiple regression learners. This design improves robustness across noisy, real-world text while maintaining stable numerical outputs suitable for long-term time-series aggregation.

The outputs of this system are not presented as raw predictions. They are normalized, aggregated, and aligned temporally so they can be directly integrated into geospatial maps, safety indicators, traffic estimation, and demand forecasting workflows used elsewhere in the platform.

Note: Text sources, labeling strategies, and scoring thresholds are intentionally abstracted to protect commercial applicability and deployment integrity.

Key Features

Transformer-Based Sentiment Encoding

Uses frozen transformer embeddings to convert raw text into dense semantic representations suitable for regression-based sentiment scoring.

Hybrid Ensemble Regression

Combines linear, tree-based, and gradient-boosted models with a meta-learner to reduce variance and stabilize predictions.

Temporal & Regional Aggregation

Aggregates sentiment and crisis signals over time and aligns them to district-level summaries suitable for time-series analysis.

Digital Twin Integration

Designed to plug directly into the same map and simulation UI used by the tourism digital twin and forecasting system.

Results & Engineering Impact

The ensemble-based sentiment modeling approach produces more stable and reliable outputs than single-model NLP pipelines. Transformer embeddings capture semantic nuance, while the regression ensemble smooths out volatility caused by short texts, sarcasm, and inconsistent language use.

From an ML engineering standpoint, freezing the language model and training lightweight downstream learners significantly reduces training cost while preserving expressive power. This makes the system suitable for periodic retraining and large-scale historical backfills without excessive compute.

On the system design side, the project emphasizes separation between language understanding and decision logic. The NLP layer produces normalized scores, while downstream components handle aggregation, trend analysis, and visualization. This allows the same sentiment intelligence to support forecasting, safety scoring, and scenario simulation without duplication.

When integrated into the tourism digital twin, these outputs act as a behavioral and perception signal that complements numerical indicators such as traffic, weather, and demand. This multi-modal fusion improves realism and interpretability for planners and analysts.

Overall, the project highlights strong software engineering discipline: modular pipelines, reproducible training, model-agnostic interfaces, and clear boundaries between ML inference and application logic. The result is a reusable intelligence component rather than a one-off sentiment model.
