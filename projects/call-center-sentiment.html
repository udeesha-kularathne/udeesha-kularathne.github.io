Multilingual Call Center Sentiment Intelligence & Advisory System

A real-time AI system that transcribes multilingual customer calls, detects emotional sentiment, and generates context-aware advisory guidance to support call center agents during live interactions.

Role ML Engineer & System Architect
Timeline 2024 – 2025
Category AI/ML, Speech Intelligence

Project Overview

This project was designed to address the limitations of traditional call center analytics, where emotional insight is typically extracted after calls have ended. The system introduces a real-time intelligence pipeline capable of understanding customer sentiment while conversations are still active.

Incoming voice segments are automatically transcribed using multilingual speech-to-text models that support Sinhala, English, and Tamil. The transcribed text is then routed through language-aware sentiment analysis pipelines, producing emotionally contextualized interpretations rather than simple polarity labels.

Sentiment outputs are combined with the original message content and passed into an AI-driven advisory model. This component generates professional, situational guidance aimed at helping call center agents respond effectively—whether the goal is de-escalation, reassurance, or reinforcement of positive interactions.

Note: Training data sources, internal thresholds, and response policies are intentionally abstracted to respect privacy, operational security, and commercial constraints.

Key Features

Multilingual Speech Transcription

Converts live audio into text using Whisper-based models capable of handling Sinhala, English, and Tamil without manual language selection.

Language-Aware Sentiment Analysis

Routes transcribed text through language-specific sentiment pipelines to ensure accurate emotional interpretation across linguistic and cultural contexts.

Ensemble-Based Sentiment Classification

Combines linear models, tree-based learners, and neural networks into a calibrated ensemble optimized for robustness in noisy, real-world conversational data.

Contextual Advice Generation

Uses a transformer-based text generation model to produce professional, emotion-aware advisory guidance tailored to the customer’s sentiment and message intent.

Low-Latency Real-Time Design

Designed to operate within tight latency budgets, ensuring insights and advice are delivered during active conversations without disrupting natural dialogue flow.

Modular & Extensible Architecture

Separates transcription, sentiment inference, and advice generation into independent services, enabling safe iteration and future model upgrades.

Results & Engineering Impact

The key outcome of this project is the successful deployment of a real-time, multilingual intelligence pipeline capable of operating within live call center environments. By combining speech recognition, sentiment inference, and advice generation into a unified workflow, the system shifts customer analytics from retrospective reporting to in-call decision support.

From a machine learning standpoint, the project demonstrates how ensemble-based sentiment modeling improves robustness when dealing with noisy conversational text produced by speech-to-text systems. The use of multiple model families allows the system to remain stable across language variations, transcription artifacts, and emotionally charged speech, where single-model approaches often degrade.

The integration of sentiment outputs with an advice generation model extends the system beyond passive emotion detection. Instead of simply labeling a customer as angry or dissatisfied, the platform translates emotional and semantic context into actionable, professional guidance that supports de-escalation, reassurance, or positive reinforcement during the conversation itself.

From an engineering perspective, the system emphasizes strict separation of concerns across the processing pipeline. Speech transcription, sentiment inference, and advice generation are implemented as isolated services connected through well-defined APIs. This design allows each component to be independently optimized, replaced, or scaled without cascading changes across the platform.

The real-time constraints of call center interactions influenced several architectural decisions, including lightweight preprocessing, batched inference strategies, and careful model selection to balance accuracy with latency. These considerations ensure that insights are delivered quickly enough to remain useful to agents without introducing disruptive delays in conversation flow.

Overall, the project demonstrates a production-oriented approach to applied AI, where machine learning models are treated as evolving system components rather than isolated experiments. The result is a scalable foundation that can be extended to additional languages, sentiment dimensions, policy-aware response generation, or deeper analytics without requiring a full architectural redesign.
