Project Overview

TasteMind was designed to move beyond star ratings and surface-level reviews by extracting structured intelligence from unstructured restaurant feedback. The system ingests real-world reviews, transforms them into sentiment and aspect-level signals, and converts those signals into interpretable scores and personalized recommendations.

The pipeline begins with automated review collection from Google Places, followed by text cleaning, normalization, and embedding-based representation. Multiple sentiment models are evaluated and combined to improve robustness, while aspect extraction decomposes reviews into dimensions such as food quality, service, ambience, pricing, and cleanliness.

A reinforcement learning layer refines recommendations over time by observing user interactions and feedback, allowing the system to adapt preferences rather than rely on static scoring rules. The frontend presents insights through dashboards, rankings, and explainable breakdowns, while backend services keep model logic decoupled from UI concerns.

Note: Review sources, weighting strategies, and learning policies are abstracted to respect platform and commercial constraints.

Key Features

Automated Review Ingestion

Fetches and standardizes live restaurant reviews from Google Places, enabling continuous data updates without manual input.

Sentiment Classification

Uses supervised NLP models to determine overall sentiment polarity and confidence, forming the foundation for higher-level scoring.

Aspect-Based Scoring

Breaks reviews into interpretable dimensions (food, service, ambience, value) and computes per-aspect sentiment scores.

Reinforcement Learning Feedback Loop

Learns user preferences over time by adjusting recommendation weights based on interaction outcomes and implicit feedback.

Explainable Recommendations

Surfaces why a restaurant is recommended by exposing aspect contributions rather than opaque overall scores.

Results & Engineering Impact

TasteMind demonstrates how unstructured text can be transformed into structured, explainable business intelligence. The combination of sentiment classification, aspect-level scoring, and reinforcement learning produces recommendations that are both adaptive and interpretable.

From an engineering perspective, the project highlights scalable data ingestion, modular ML services, and a clear separation between model logic and presentation. This design supports extension to new review sources, additional aspects, or alternative learning strategies without reworking the core system.
