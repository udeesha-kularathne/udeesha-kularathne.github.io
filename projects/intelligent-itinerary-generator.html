Intelligent Itinerary Generator

A production-oriented AI system that converts tourism forecasts, sentiment intelligence, risk signals, and user preferences into optimized, adaptive, day-by-day travel itineraries delivered through a mobile-first experience.

Role AI Engineer & Mobile Systems Engineer
Timeline 2024 – 2025
Category AI/ML, Optimization, Mobile App

Project Overview

This project represents the final decision layer of a larger smart tourism platform. While upstream components focus on forecasting demand and extracting sentiment and risk intelligence, this system is responsible for converting those signals into concrete, personalized travel plans.

The itinerary generator combines hybrid machine learning, constrained optimization, geospatial reasoning, and behavioral adaptation to produce realistic, human-friendly itineraries rather than simple ranked lists. The system is designed to operate under real-world constraints such as time, distance, cost, category diversity, and regional cohesion.

Outputs are delivered through a React Native (Expo) mobile interface featuring interactive maps, route visualization, and daily summaries, ensuring that complex backend intelligence is translated into an intuitive user experience.

Note: Exact weighting strategies, thresholds, and operational heuristics are abstracted to preserve commercial viability and deployment flexibility.

Key Features

Hybrid Preference Modeling

Uses an ensemble of gradient-boosted trees and a fusion neural network to estimate user–attraction compatibility scores.

Context-Aware Decision Logic

Integrates forecasts, sentiment intelligence, safety signals, and weather conditions to adjust recommendations dynamically.

Diversity & Region-Aware Clustering

Organizes attractions into daily plans that balance category diversity, geographic proximity, and feasible travel time.

Behavioral Adaptation Loop

Tracks deviations between planned and actual behavior to discover new user interests and refine future itineraries.

Technology & Engineering Stack

Python XGBoost Fusion Neural Networks Negative Sampling Constraint Optimization Geospatial Reasoning Route Optimization React Native (Expo) TypeScript Mobile UI Engineering Node.js Service-Oriented APIs System Integration

Results & Engineering Impact

The hybrid preference modeling approach produces significantly more coherent itineraries than rule-based or ranking-only systems. By learning from both positive and negative user–attraction interactions, the model can distinguish between acceptable and genuinely preferred experiences rather than simply selecting popular locations.

From an ML systems perspective, this project demonstrates how predictive models can be safely embedded into constraint-heavy environments. Model outputs are treated as soft signals that are refined through optimization logic, ensuring feasibility with respect to time, distance, cost, and diversity requirements.

On the software engineering side, the system emphasizes modular design and clear boundaries between intelligence layers. Forecasting, sentiment, risk analysis, itinerary generation, routing, and behavioral learning operate as independent components connected through well-defined interfaces, enabling maintainability and extensibility.

The mobile frontend focuses on interpretability and usability rather than raw prediction exposure. Map-based visualization, route polylines, and daily summaries help users understand and trust the recommendations, which is essential for real-world adoption.

Overall, this project demonstrates end-to-end AI product engineering: transforming data into intelligence, intelligence into decisions, and decisions into a polished user experience. It reflects the design patterns used in modern decision-support and personalization systems.
